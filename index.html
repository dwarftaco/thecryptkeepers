Why We Exist

The Order of the Cryptkeepers stands as guardians of memory and servants of the Goddess of Death's eternal cycle. We exist to create sacred spaces of reverence for the departed, to preserve the history of our people through names and epitaphs, and to strengthen our community through the bonds of remembrance.

In Imperia's great events, we are the keepers of dignity, ensuring that no soul passes into the beyond without honor, and no name is lost to the passage of time.

Our Sacred Goals

Through devotion to the Goddess of Death and reverence for her eternal cycle, we undertake two sacred missions that will define our legacy in Imperia.

  • Build a Graveyard & Crypt
    We shall construct a modest graveyard with a central church and mausoleum, complete with underground crypts. This sacred ground will provide space for citizens to honor the dead through funerals, rites, and processions, all rooted in reverence for the Goddess of Death and the natural cycle of life.
  • Keep a Ledger of the Dead
    In our church, we will maintain a sacred record of every citizen who passes away including their name, date, cause of death, and a meaningful epitaph crafted by reaching out to those who knew them. This living archive honors memory and history, creating a digital ledger that ensures no life is forgotten.

Our Core Values

These sacred principles guide every action we take and every stone we lay. They are the foundation upon which our Order stands, and the light that guides us in service to the Goddess of Death.

  • Reverence
    We honor the dead and the Goddess who guides them through the eternal cycle, treating each soul with the dignity they deserve.
  • Memory
    No life is forgotten, each name is preserved. Through our ledgers and epitaphs, we ensure that every citizen's story endures.
  • Community
    Rites and remembrance bring the living together, strengthening the bonds that unite us in both grief and celebration of life.
  • Dignity
    Every funeral, epitaph, and procession is treated with the utmost respect, for in death we are all equal before the Goddess.
